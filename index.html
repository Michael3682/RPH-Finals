About Catarman

Barangay Catarman is part of the municipality of Cordova in Cebu, Philippines. The history of Catarman is intertwined with that of Cordova, which was originally established as a separate municipality on May 22, 1863, by Governor General Rafael Echague. This period of independence was short-lived, as Cordova was re-annexed to Opon (now Lapu-Lapu City) during the American colonial period. It regained its municipal status on January 1, 1913, through an executive order by US Governor General Newton W. Gilbert (CORDOVA CEBU) (The History Of Cordova).

Catarman, as one of the barangays of Cordova, has developed over the years along with the municipality. The area is characterized by its coastal location, with some regions at an elevation of approximately 6.7 meters above sea level. As of the 2020 Census, Catarman had a population of 5,844 people (PhilAtlas).
